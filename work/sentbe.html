Sentbe

Sentbe is a fin-tech startup located in Seoul, Korea. During my rapid 6 week roadmap, I worked on a mobile application that simplifies the international transfer process.

The projects I worked on are as follows:

  1. Conducting competitor research across 20+ international remittance companies to contribute in an annual report
  2. Conducting UX research to suggest a possible redesign guideline that improves the overall user experience
  3. Designing a promotional website for an oversees remittance mobile app

My Role

I worked as the lead Competitor Researcher and created the research templates and guidelines. I also lead the initial stages of the Redesign Project until the termination of my internship. For the Promotional Website Project, my main role was UX Writing and content design.

Project 2: App Redesign

I was part of the initial stage of the redesign project. As the design lead, I presented a redesign idea of the current mobile app based on the insights from the competitor analysis and further user research. Because I had a limited amount of time, I narrowed my UX research methods into three.

Methods

Developing main user persona based on the previous company research.

Identifying common issues throughout international remittance apps by scanning the reviews of the Sentbe app as well as competing apps.

Understanding and Defining problem spaces through user interviews.

Secondary Research & Interviews

I was able to recruit 4 interview participants. 2 of them were foreign workers while the others were students studying in the United States. Interviewing them helped me discover usability problems specific to Sentbe. Meanwhile, the secondary research was helpful with discovering general issues of international remittance apps.

After these activities, I categorized the problems into two. Because I began this project a week before the termination of the internship, I was only able to focus on the first problem type.

Key Takeaways
  • Ask as many questions as possible!
  • Communication is key. Be articulate and clear when explaining designs to other teams.
  • Don't be afraid to speak up and express opinions. Often times, the ideas and opinions will be cherished. Even if the contribution wasn't so significant, there is always something to learn from it.
  • Show instead of tell. But always be prepared to articulate my designs clearly with ample reasons.
