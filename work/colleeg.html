Colleeg

How might we provide affordable help to high school students during the college admission process?

Statistically, more students from affluent homes get into more elite colleges—without cheating. This is because a lot of the stronger college applications are likely to have gone through the hands of expensive private counselors. Since private counseling is so prevalent in the college admissions process, I decided to create a platform that makes private counseling more open-sourced and accessible. The solution I generated as a result was a website that connects high school students with college mentors.

My Role

Although this was an individual project, I was constantly asking for feedback from my peers to get more diverse opinions.

UX Methods

Secondary Research · Survey Design · User Interviews · User Testing · User Flow Mapping · Wireframing · Prototyping · Visual Design

Problem

It is extremely difficult for high school students to finish college applications on their own. Meanwhile, private college counseling is highly inaccessible and ineffective for its price.

Challenge

How can I make a functioning service structure?

Solution

A web app that helps high schoolers discover college mentors to receive personalized help at a reasonable price.

SOLUTION OVERVIEW

1. Personalized Search

Various layers of filters allow high schoolers to discover mentors efficiently. Each profile card is designed to display enough context about the mentor's availability before clicking on them to see more details.

2. Schedule a Meeting

Students can schedule a meeting with a college mentor of their choice. The meeting planner makes the mentees provide enough information about their needs. This allows mentors to prepare for the session in advance and lead the sessions smoothly.

3. Access & Manage Meeting Sessions

Students can quickly access and manage upcoming meetings.

4. Report Issues, Send Feedback

Since the college students are not trained professionals, many users were concerned with problematic behaviors that might show during the 1:1 sessions. To prevent this, I included a systematic review & refund system.

5. Improve Mentoring, Get Paid

Mentors are able to view analytics of the mentees' feedback. The detail overview of the Reports made by the mentees will make the mentors more self-aware of their shortcomings and improve. Mentors can also see the details of each Report Case - specifically the No-show cases - and reschedule meetings to fulfill their responsibilities.
The My Tickets page shows all the Tickets that have been accumulated through successful 1:1 sessions. The mentors can use this page to transfer their Tickets into real money or transfer them into gift cards.

DESIGN PROCESS
UNDERSTAND

Background Research

More Students Use Private College Counselors than People Think.

First, I decided to conduct secondary research to understand how the college admission process is currently being done. One piece of information that stood out to me the most was that more than 14% of students at Ivy League come from families in the top 1% of the income distribution. And most of them, and many others, were getting help from a private college counselor. This essentially meant that students who do not come from an affluent background have a lower chance of getting into their dream schools because wealthy students' applications are more likely to stand out.

Learning about this problem made me set my project goal: "How can I give every high school student an opportunity to receive guidance and stand out as a college applicant?"

UNDERSTAND

Surveys & Interviews

I decided to take a deep dive into the topic of private college counseling. Are they effective? What do students think about private counseling?

According to a survey I sent out to high school students who were using private counseling, 7 out of 8 respondants thought that private counseling was not as helpful as they initially thought it would be. To learn more about the source of this problem, user pain points, and their needs, I interviewed 3 high school students and 3 college students who had private counseling experience.

UNDERSTAND

Insights

Here are the key insights I gathered through an affinity diagram exercise after the user interviews

Finding a matching private counselor takes time

Considering the time it takes to search for a counselor, meet with them, and come to an understanding, the entire process was very inefficient.

Overwhelming price vs. underwhelming results

Most students weren't fully aware of the prices, but they estimated the price to be more than $10,000. However, the end-results were not what they expected from the price.

Students want to know specific, insider, information about the major of the schools.

Students had the most challenging time writing major-specific essays. However, the private counselors didn't provide enough reliable information to help students write a stellar essay.

Getting help from a family member in college was the most helpful

However, not all students have a friend or a family member in college who have the dedication to help.

UNDERSTAND

Competitive Analysis

At this point, I already had a rough idea about the solution: a platform that helps high school students discover mentors in a specific college. However, there are many platforms out there that already provide a similar service. I decided to conduct a competitive analysis and analyze the problems and see what I can learn from them.

ADPList (a platform that pairs design job-seekers with an established designer working in the industry) had the best results. However, their Availability, Responsiveness, and Efficiency criteria were lacking because:

  1. Mentors don't get compensated, making them commit less.
  2. Out of hundreds of mentor profiles, only a few of them are actually available within the same month. Most of the time, they won't put up their availability and ask the mentees to reach out through email—which has a 50/50 chance of getting a response.
  3. Mentees don't get to view the mentor's availability until they click on the profile—a very inefficient way to discover available mentors.

DESIGN & TEST

Iterations

After the 2nd user test, my new challenge was to design a better review & refund system to prevent high school students from having a negative experience. I also had to come up with an appealing value proposition for college students. As a result, I came up with the following:

Review & Refund

After each 1:1 session, high school students will be asked to leave a review. However, posting a review may feel like a lot of work to many. To encourage users to give feedback, I tried to make the process as simple and informative as possible.

Reviews on Mentor Profile

The reviews will be reflected on mentors' profiles. High school students will decide whether to schedule a meeting with a mentor or not based on their discretion of the reviews. When users hover on the Schedule button, information about the use of the Tickets will pop up.

Mentor's View

This is what the college mentors will see on the website. In My Sessions, the Analytics will display a summary of feedbacks from the mentees. This will reduce various issues such as the "no-show" since mentors become more self-aware of their coaching style.

Your Tickets page will show the accumulated amount of Tickets that mentors received from successful 1:1 sessions. I added emphasis on the Ticket-to-real money conversion and the "Redeem As Gift Card" feature to motivate the mentors to use the platform more.

DESIGN

Final Prototype

The user experience focuses on 4 things that my target users valued the most: personalization, efficiency, accessibility, and reliability.

Personalized Search

As shown in the solution overview, various filters allow high schoolers to discover the exact type of mentor they need. Students can sort the mentors by their school, major, type of help they need, grade, availability, and the number of reviews.

Efficiency

The process of finding an available mentor and scheduling a meeting is designed to be time-efficient. Note that the "Meeting Request Response Rate" shows up when the mentor has not posted his/her availability for immediate scheduling. This provides enough context about the mentor's schedule before clicking on the card.

The meeting planner makes the mentees provide enough information about their needs. This allows mentors to prepare for the session in advance and lead the sessions smoothly.

Accessibility

Ideally, students should be able to schedule a meeting with a mentor of their choice (Ex: a mentor from a specific school studying a specific major). In real-life settings, schedules might not align, and that perfect mentor may not be available at the moment. The 'Request Schedule' feature prevents this kind of situation.

Reliability

Since the college students are not trained professionals, a lot of people were concerned with problematic behaviors that might show during the 1:1 sessions. To prevent this, I included a systematic review & refund system.

Mentors' View

Mentors are able to view analytics of the mentees' feedback. The detail overview of the Reports made by the mentees will make the mentors more self-aware of their shortcomings and improve. Mentors can also see the details of each Report Case - specifically the No-show cases - and reschedule meetings that have been missed.

The My Tickets page shows all the Tickets that have been accumulated through successful 1:1 sessions. The mentors can use this page to transfer their Tickets into real money or transfer them into gift cards.
