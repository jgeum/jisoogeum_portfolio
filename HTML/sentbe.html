Sentbe

Sentbe is a Korean fin-tech company specializing in oversees remittance. The main service is provided through a mobile application and a website, and their function is to allow users working in South Korea to easily send money overseas with lower transfer rates, service fees, etc. The service is done through a few simple clicks and a verification process that users only have to go through once.

During my time here, I worked on several projects:
1. Designing a promotional website for an oversees remittance mobile app.
2. Conducting competitor research across 20+ international remittance companies for annual report.
3. Designing a concept app based on the competitor research

Project 1: Competitor Research

I lead the competitor research project and presented my discoveries, insights and suggestions to the product team. The final report included detail analysis of digital platforms from 20+ competitor companies, high-level overview of their business structure, and key insights/suggestions.

To analyze digital platforms, I first created a research template that would outline the areas of focus such as the on-boarding and the bank account verification process. I then tested different platforms with other interns using the template.
I often referred to TechCrunch and other online sources to analyze business models.

Project 2: App Redesign

I presented a redesign of the current mobile application based on the insights from the competitor analysis and further user research.

Research

I first looked at user ratings/reviews of similar apps and compare them with the ones from Sentbe. This allowed me to discover common usability issues across international remittance apps.

To discover usability problems specific to Sentbe, I decided to conduct a guerrilla user test with 4 participants who fit the user profile (foreigner working in SK or international students).

Problem 1: Poor navigation toward/lack of awareness about the in-app customer support center.

Customer support center is usually well-established for most international remittance services due to the high demand. This is because users tend to get irritated very quickly when unexpected things occur as the scale of transactions tend to be larger than domestic transfers. However, many of the issues mentioned in user reviews had some connection with poor customer support navigation experience.

Problem 2: Most users are unaware of the promotional opportunities.

Promotion banners are hidden inside the See More page, which is often neglected by the users. Most didn't even bother to look for any deals or opportunities unless they were asked to.

Problem 3: Users forget that they need to charge the Wallet in order to begin the transfer process.

In order for the transfer process to even begin, users must manually deposit money to Sentbe's Wallet. However, many did not realize this and expected the transaction to begin immediately because users already had to authorize their bank account information with the app during the on-boarding process.

An ideal solution for this would be to remove the Wallet feature and make the transactions automatic. However, this solution was not a viable option as the Korean government currently bans automatic transactions between international transfers.

I eventually realized that the misunderstanding actually comes from the lack of visual emphasis and hierarchy in the Review & confirm transaction page.

Solutions

After understanding the pain points and their causes, I came up with three solutions.

1. Create a separate screen dedicated to customer support.
2. Create a dashboard to remind users about important information, such as deposit reminders.
The dashboard can also be a space to promote special deals and seasonal events.
3. Refine information architecture and visual hierarchy throughout the app.

Final Designs

1. Customer Support Center

As mentioned above, in-app Customer Support is a popular feature in international remittance apps. Thus, I brought the customer support center to the bottom menu to bring more attention to this feature.

2. Home / Dashboard

The Send Money screen can now be accessed by clicking the Send Money button in the new home screen OR clicking the middle button on the bottom menu. The addition of a home / dashboard screen serves several benefits. First, it makes it easier to display new features or events. Second, users can view all the available features at a glance. Third, it provides more room to insert important reminders and notifications.

3. Refined Information Architecture

I restructured the information in the "Detailed History" screen in the order of importance.

The original "History" screen shows the transaction history between the user and the recipient, while the original "Wallet" screen shows the history of transaction from the user's private bank account to Sentbe's virtual account. I thought that these features could be organized in the same category so I combined the screens and used tabs to differentiate the content.

I removed the Promotions and Customer Service sections from the See More page since they were moved to different screens in my redesign.

Project 3: Promotional Website

The last project was a quick design sprint. The marketing team wanted to create a short and concise promotional webpage - a common advertising format in South Korea. The marketing team initially provided us with all the writing content for the webpage, but we ended up changing it up in the process.

My Role

One of my roles in this project was UX writing. Originally, the marketing team who assigned the project had specific wordings and descriptions planned out. Most of the original texts were the same as the ones from Sentbe's main website. Ex) "Think Outside the Bank"

However, I decided to change the text to communicate our features more clearly.

Throughout the website, I tried to come up with words that would describe the product most efficiently, without errors. In terms of design, My teammates and I came up with different iterations of each section and collaboratively decided on which one to choose.

Key Takeaways
  • Ask as many questions as possible!
  • Communication is key. Be articulate and clear when explaining designs to other teams.
  • Don't be afraid to speak up and express opinions. Often times, the ideas and opinions will be cherished. Even if the contribution wasn't so significant, there is always something to learn from it.
  • Show instead of tell. But always be prepared to articulate my designs clearly with ample reasons.
