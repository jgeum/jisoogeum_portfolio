Construction Junction

Construction Junction is a large non-profit warehouse that sells surplus construction materials and appliances at reduced prices. My teammates and I designed a mobile app that promotes CJ's business, which is increasing the number of sales and donation for reused furnitures.

Through user research, we found out that the core problem areas were:
1. Not having an online space to display products, which make potential customers turn to other businesses with an online platform.
2. Confusing donation instructions and process, which make potential donors turn to unsustainable options.

Our Solution

A mobile app that:
1. Provides step-by-step instructions for the material donation process.
2. Allows furniture seekers to shop used furnitures online
3. Promotes CJ's activities and events

View Interactive Prototype
SOLUTION OVERVIEW

Donation Wizard

1. Choose the type of item to donate

Begin the donation process by choosing the type of item you wish to donate.

2. Scan your item

Scan the item using the camera to check the quality of your item. The system will review the donation eligibility list required by Construction Junction, and compare it to your item.

3. Additional questions

Answer simple questions to clarify some of the areas of your item that the camera could not detect. The questions will be curated for the particular type of item you have selected to donate.

4. Schedule

Schedule a pickup time or a drop-off time. Scheduling a pickup service will inform the Construction Junction employees about any relevant information. Scheduling a drop-off service will also let the employees know when you are arriving to the warehouse, so they can properly keep track of the inventory.

UNDERSTAND

Background Research

Get to know our client

CJ's mission is to support and promote conservation through the reuse of building materials. However, CJ's sales depend highly on used materials provided by donors and various types of customers.
As a non-profit organization, CJ plays an important role in supporting many other communities in Pittsburgh. For instance, CJ provides employment training programs with Goodwill and assists local social service, environmental, and art focused nonprofits through the donation of building material and furniture.

Who are the stakeholders?

We first decided to conduct some background research on the people who are involved with the service. Online information about CJ was very helpful for our research, but a short interview with the employees helped us understand more about the value flow, the customers, and the stakeholders.

UNDERSTAND

Pain Points

Although we were able to get an idea of few of the problems through background research, we still had to identify the exact pain points. To do that, we talked to a few customers as well as non-customers in order to fully understand the customer pain points.

The Pain Points

Feels that it's risky to drive all the way to CJ's warehouse without having prior knowledge about the inventory and the quality of items. As a result, they turn to alternative services such as Ikea or Amazon.

"Why should I drive all the way to CJ's warehouse to donate when I can conveniently get rid of my old furnitures nearby?"

Many donors are unaware of the donation pick-up service and those who do are unsure about the strict requirements for donations.

Frustrated by the amount of broken furnitures being donated into the warehouse.

Wishes to increase donations and sales.

Wants more locals to know about CJ's activities such as fundraisers, volunteer programs, and many other community outreach programs.

DEFINE

Interviews

Problem with the donation process

We realized most of the problems raised by our stakeholders and customers are related to the donation process. We decided to look more into the steps that are required for furniture donation, and found out that it is more complicated than we expected.
Below is the list of requirements that each types of donation materials should meet in order to be kept in the warehouse. The list is extremely long, hard to read, and hard to find. This minor inconvenience is the reason why many potential donors end up using other services. Furthermore, those who still decide to donate for CJ were submitting ineligible materials, putting the CJ workers in a difficult position.

Ideate

Brainstorm Solutions

To solve this issue, we envisioned implementing a mobile feature that would guide donors through the eligibility list as well as the whole donation process without making it feel tedious. Since we want the users to self-inspect the condition of their own donation materials, we decided to design a mobile 3D scanning feature that detects any problem areas of a physical object.

Below is a guide on how a mobile 3D scanning application works.

Solution

It was crucial for donors to know the proper ways of donating materials at CJ. Simultaneously, CJ was in need of an online inventory in order to attract customers at any place and time. By bringing these problem spaces together, we came up with a mobile app that can promote and improve CJ's service.

Easy donation process with a step-by-step Donation Wizard

Browse second-hand furnitures at home through the Mobile Inventory

Get informed about CJ's activities and support furniture reuse

Although the solution includes 3 different features, our group decided to only prototype the Donation Wizard. The Donation Wizard seemed like the most interesting and challenging solution to focus our time on, since the other solutions can always follow the industry conventions (a product browsing page and a detail pages).

DESIGN & TEST

Wireframes

Below is the first wireframe for the Donation Wizard. Our wireframe initially encouraged users to read the donation eligibility list (with a refined information architecture) before moving into the actual donation process. However, we realized that this method is not so different from the original website. To improve the user experience, we came up with a solution that skips this tedious process while indirectly informing the users of the eligibility list. In our case, making users to 3D scan the furnitures and displaying only the relevant eligibility information - for example, indicating red flags if the item is heavily damaged - seemed like the best solution.

REFLECTION

Future Opportunities To Be Explored

As mentioned in the "Solutions," our group focused on designing the best user experience for the Donation Wizard since it seemed like the most challenging one to design. However, the other solutions also offer some unique design opportunities to be explored.

Opportunity 1: Community Board

According to the CJ employees who participated in our primary research, CJ wanted to increase community engagement and promote fundraising events. Our next design opportunity would be to find a way to promote these events for both frequent users and the outside community members.

Opportunity 2: Product Browsing Page

Currently, CJ does not support online shopping, which is a huge pain point to customers who have to drive all the way to the warehouse. Our next design opportunity would be implementing an AR feature that allows buyers to see how CJ furnitures would look like in their house before visiting CJ.
